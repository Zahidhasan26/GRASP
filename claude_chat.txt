Identified critical power misconception and ADC gap requiring external hardware

Before the guide — two corrections that will save you hours tonight.
The ESP32 was never the problem
Its GPIO pins only send the PWM signal; they were never meant to power the servos. The architecture already called for a separate 5–6 V rail with a common ground. The Pi has exactly the same limitation — its GPIO can't drive servos either. If you wire it the same way, you'll get the same brownouts.
The real culprit is almost certainly the 9 V battery. A 9 V block has tiny current capacity and high internal resistance; two MG90S servos pull ~700 mA–1 A each at stall. The rail collapses, the microcontroller browns out, and it looks like the MCU is at fault. Also — MG90S servos are rated 4.8–6 V. Feeding them 9 V will cook them. Check what you actually have.
But switch to the Pi anyway — it's the better call
Not for power. For three real reasons: it serves the website itself (no laptop needed), it can run Gemini Live audio natively, and it's a real Linux box you can debug.
⚠️ The catch nobody mentions: the Pi has no ADC. The ESP32 had one built in. The MyoWare's ENV output is analog. You need an MCP3008 (SPI, ~$4, in every hobby shop). Without it, the Pi cannot read your muscle sensor at all. This is the single most important thing in this message.
